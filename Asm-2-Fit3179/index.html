Cost of Living

Australia is the 6th Most Expensive Country in the World

Cost of Living Index (Excl. Rent) is a
relative indicator of consumer goods prices,
including groceries, restaurants,transportation
and utilities. Cost of Living Index does not
includes accommodation expenses such as rent
or mortgage.
If a country has a Cost of Living Index of
120, it means Numbeo has estimated it is
20% more expensive than New York
(excluding rent)

Cost of services for Australia

The cost of insurance and financial
services exhibits a pronounced
annual growth, as illustrated by
the steep upward trajectory in
the line chart.
The cost of maintenance demonstrates
minimal fluctuations, as reflected
in the marginal changes depicted
in the chart.

Annual increase in rent

Rental prices rose 6.7 per cent annually.
This is the largest annual rise since 2009,
reflecting low vacancy rates amid a tight
rental market across the country.
